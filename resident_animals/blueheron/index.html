Great Blue Heron

Description

The Great Blue Heron has long legs, a strong dager-like bill, and a shaggy appearance due to the plumes around its head, chest and wings. They are blue-gray in coloring and have a black stripe over the eye. Most notably they can be identified by the “S” shape made by its neck.

Habitat

Great Blue Herons are commonly found throughout Minnesota in the warmer months and in the southern areas, year round. While their habitat ranges a variety of environments, they are likely to be spotted along marshes, riverbanks and freshwater lakes.

Great Blue Heron
